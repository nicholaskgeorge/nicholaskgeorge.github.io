Sailvane Research Project

This research project involved constructing an autonomous solar powered sailboat to be used to collect weather data in the ocean. The boat is intended to be designed to be low powered enough to sail on the ocean for months without the need to be interacted with. I work on this project under the guidance of professor Andy Ruina from the school of mechanical engineering. I work in the lab with two grad school students.

Project Backround

As stated the main goal of the project was to make an autonmous sail boat in order to collect weather data. There were several inovations put into the boat in order to acomplish the low power requirments of the boat. One special thing of our boat is that the rudder is placed in the air rather than in the water.

Placing the rudder in the air rather than the water adds what is known as automated stability into the boat. If the boat goes off course it will automatically adjust to go back into the direction of the wind without any movement of onboard actuator, which saves power. In addition to this, the hardware of the boat has also been chosen in order to be as low powered as possible. The main computer on board which controls the boat is a microcontroller called the SAME70. This microcontroller is designed to have have a low power mode, so that we can essentially turn of the microcontroller for short periods during deep sea explroation and turn it back on when higher computation is needed.

The boat was also designed to communicate through three different methods. The first was a simple remote control receiver, which would let the boat be controlled through direct remote control. The second is a radio module, which lets the boat receive explicit commands from a base station computer up to several miles away. The last means of communication is through satalite, where the boat can send out satalite messages to our base station computer from anywhere on the plant. These three different communication methods give a lot of different mediums to communicate with the boat.

Acomplishments

Acomplishments

In this project, my job has been to manage and program the onboard embedded system for the boat, do all the wiring and design the software for the boat. I have succefully programmed the microcontroller to extract data from the different sensors on the board, use the actuators and facilitate wireless communication with the boat. I have also implemented the beginings of a short navigation algorithm in order to do simple point-to-point navigation

With my guidance the project is still moving along with steady progress. My and the other grad students in the lab are developing the boat in order to soon do preliminary test in one of the pools at Cornell. Recently I have been working closley to design an I/O pannel for the boat, decoding serial data for the remote control, designing a new program scheduler to organize all of the functions on the boat and doing more water tests to check for leaks. Progress is slowley being made and we hope to do a whole boat test soon to test the onboard algorithm

Sailvane Research Project

This research project involved constructing an autonomous solar powered sailboat to be used to collect weather data in the ocean. The boat is intended to be designed to be low powered enough to sail on the ocean for months without the need to be interacted with. I work on this project under the guidance of professor Andy Ruina from the school of mechanical engineering. I work in the lab with two grad school students.

Project Backround

As stated the main goal of the project was to make an autonmous sail boat in order to collect weather data. There were several inovations put into the boat in order to acomplish the low power requirments of the boat. One special thing of our boat is that the rudder is placed in the air rather than in the water.

Placing the rudder in the air rather than the water adds what is known as automated stability into the boat. If the boat goes off course it will automatically adjust to go back into the direction of the wind without any movement of onboard actuator, which saves power.
